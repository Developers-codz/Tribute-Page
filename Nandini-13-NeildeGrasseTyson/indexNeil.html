NEIL DEGRASSE TYSON
American Astrophysicist, Cosmologist, Planetary Scientist, Author, and Science Communicator



We are part of this universe; we are in this universe, but perhaps more important than both of those facts, is that the universe is in us.

  • Neil deGrasse Tyson wrote monthly essays in the "Universe" column for Natural History magazine, some of which were later published in his books 'Death by Black Hole' and 'Astrophysics for People in a Hurry'.

  • In 1994, he joined the Hayden Planetarium as a staff scientist and the Princeton faculty as a visiting research scientist and lecturer. In 1996, he became director of the planetarium and oversaw its $210 million reconstruction project, which was completed in 2000.

  • Tyson served on a 2001 government commission on the future of the U.S. aerospace industry and on the 2004 Moon, Mars and Beyond commission.

  • He was awarded the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal.

  • From 2006 to 2011, he hosted the television show NOVA ScienceNow on PBS. Since 2009, Tyson has hosted the weekly podcast StarTalk.

  • In 2014, he hosted the television series Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey, a successor to Carl Sagan's 1980 series Cosmos: A Personal Voyage

  • The U.S. National Academy of Sciences awarded Tyson the Public Welfare Medal in 2015 for his "extraordinary role in exciting the public about the wonders of science"
